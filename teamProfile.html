Introduction to Information Technology

How is this information helpful to the group dymamic?

Personal collaboration with others to progress through an objective requires different considerations. In a solo task, the only contributor you need to take into account is yourself. When others are involved, an understanding of their strengths, weaknesses, skills, likes, dislikes and experience which are relevant to the mutual task is detrimental to the outcomes quality. In our group collaboration’s case, the personal information (and all of each other’s work from assessment 1) adequately inform us of individual capabilities. We need to react to this information by making appropriate task delegations, finding the best way of communicating to each individual, how we can help support each other's progress, among other things.
